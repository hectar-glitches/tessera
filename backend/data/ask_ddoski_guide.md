# AI Hackathon 2026 — Official Hacker Guide (Ask Ddoski)

Welcome to AI Hackathon 2026. This guide is the single source of truth for hackers.
Ask Ddoski anything about tracks, prizes, meals, schedule, and rules.

## Tracks Overview

AI Hackathon 2026 has exactly three tracks. You may submit your project to exactly
one main track — no hedging across tracks. The three tracks are Ddoski's Lab,
Ddoski's Toolbox, and the World track.

## Ddoski's Lab

Ddoski's Lab is the research and experimentation track. It rewards novel models,
new training techniques, and bold experiments that may not be production-ready.
Judges weight originality and technical depth over polish.

## Ddoski's Toolbox

Ddoski's Toolbox is the developer-tooling and infrastructure track. It rewards
projects that make other builders faster: SDKs, agents, dev tools, and platform
infrastructure. Judges weight usefulness, craft, and how reusable the tool is.

## World Track

The World track is the real-world-impact track. It rewards projects that help a
specific community or under-resourced organization. Judges weight measurable impact
and the clarity of the problem being solved.

## Prizes

The Ddoski's Lab prize is $5,000 in cash plus research compute credits.
The Ddoski's Toolbox prize is $4,000 in cash plus a featured listing in the Ddoski
developer marketplace. The World track prize is $3,000 in cash plus a mentorship
package. Sponsor prizes are awarded separately from the main track prizes.

## Saturday Meals

On Saturday, breakfast is served at 8:00 am, Saturday lunch is served at 12:30 pm in
the main hall, and Saturday dinner is served at 7:00 pm. A late-night snack bar opens
at 11:00 pm on Saturday.

## Sunday Meals

On Sunday, brunch is served at 10:00 am in the main hall, and Sunday lunch is served
at 1:00 pm. There is no Sunday dinner — the event ends after the closing ceremony in
the afternoon.

## Schedule

Hacking opens Saturday at 9:00 am and the submission deadline is Sunday at 12:00 pm.
Opening ceremony is Saturday at 8:30 am. Closing ceremony and demos are Sunday at
3:00 pm.

## Team Size and Eligibility

Teams may have between 1 and 4 members. Solo hackers are welcome. All team members
must be registered attendees. You may not be on more than one team.

## Submissions

Submit your project on Devpost before the Sunday 12:00 pm deadline. A submission must
include a public code repository, a 3 to 4 minute demo video, and a written
description. Late submissions are not accepted.
